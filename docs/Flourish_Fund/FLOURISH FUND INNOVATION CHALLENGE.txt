FLOURISH FUND INNOVATION CHALLENGE
Technology & Innovation for Family Flourishing
________________


EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
The Flourish Fund Innovation Challenge is a $600,000 competitive funding opportunity to accelerate technology solutions in foster care and family preservation — with a potential increase to $800,000 pending a final commitment. The Challenge features a dual-track national competition: a Nonprofit Track ($500,000 funded by Google.org through Flourish Fund's Technology & Innovation Initiative) and a Social Enterprise Track ($100,000 funded by Eagle Ventures).

This first-of-its-kind structure brings nonprofits and mission-driven for-profits together under one umbrella, enabling Flourish Fund and its partners to:
* Discover innovative solutions across both nonprofit and for-profit sectors
* Support emerging organizations with fresh approaches to foster care challenges
* Build a cross-sector community of innovators working toward shared goals
* Create a pipeline of potential future Scale grant recipients and investment opportunities

CHALLENGE OVERVIEW
Name: Flourish Fund Innovation Challenge: Technology for Family Flourishing
Total Fund Pool: $600,000 (with potential increase to $800,000 — see Pending Funding below)

Dual-Track Structure:

NONPROFIT TRACK (Flourish Fund — $500,000)
* Funded by Google.org through Flourish Fund's Technology & Innovation Initiative
* Open to 501(c)(3) nonprofits and fiscally sponsored projects
* Awards structured as grants; Google.org open-source IP requirements apply

* 4 Winners: $100,000 each
* 4 Runners-Up: $25,000 each
* Total Awards: 8 organizations

Grant Flexibility:
* Winners receive 60% flexible funding ($60,000) for general operations and sustainability, plus 40% ($40,000) designated for technology innovation

SOCIAL ENTERPRISE TRACK (Eagle Ventures — $100,000)
* Funded by Eagle Ventures
* Open to mission-driven for-profits, B-Corps, and social enterprises
* Awards structured as investment or prize; no open-source IP requirements

Focus Areas: Aligned with Flourish Fund's three strategic pillars:
1. Preventing Family Separation — Digital solutions that strengthen at-risk families
2. Supporting Foster Families — Technology tools that reduce burnout and improve retention

ELIGIBILITY REQUIREMENTS

NONPROFIT TRACK
Eligible Organizations:
* 501(c)(3) nonprofits or fiscal sponsorship
* Annual revenue under $2 million
* Currently serving at least 50 families/youth annually (or clear plan to reach this within 12 months)
* Based in or serving U.S. communities
* Early-stage organizations prioritized; mid-size orgs piloting new tech also eligible

SOCIAL ENTERPRISE TRACK
Eligible Organizations:
* For-profit organizations with explicit social mission
* Registered B-Corps and benefit corporations
* Seed to Series A stage
* Annual revenue under $2 million
* Pre-revenue startups eligible
* Currently serving or planning to serve at least 50 families/youth annually

Eligible Projects (All Tracks):
* Must incorporate innovative technology solutions (or new service models with technology components)
* Directly address foster care or family preservation
* Include plan for measuring cost per outcome
* Demonstrate human-centered design approach
* Nonprofit track: commit to open-source requirements per Google.org agreement

Special Pathways:

Lived Experience Leadership:
* Organizations led by people with personal experience in foster care or family preservation systems are especially encouraged
* Brings unique insights that make solutions more effective
* Active recognition in the selection process

Organizations Over $2M Revenue:
* May submit a Letter of Inquiry (LOI) for custom funding through Flourish Fund's standard grant process
* 2-3 page maximum, including organization overview, innovation description, technology component, alignment & impact, and pilot scope (12-18 months)
* Typical funding range: $50,000-$150,000
* Rolling basis, typically 4-6 week response time

Ineligible:
* Government agencies
* Individuals without organizational affiliation
* For-profit companies without explicit social mission (nonprofit track)
* Traditional venture-backed startups prioritizing returns over impact (social enterprise track)
* Projects focused solely on research without direct service component

SELECTION CRITERIA
Applications will be evaluated on five key criteria (20 points each, 100 total):

1. INNOVATION (20 points)
* Novel application of digital tools, automation, or data analytics
* Creative use of existing technology for foster care challenges
* Potential to transform traditional service delivery models
* Clear technological differentiation from existing solutions

2. IMPACT POTENTIAL (20 points)
* Clear target population and number served
* Evidence-based theory of change
* Projected cost per outcome improvements (targeting 30% reduction)
* Potential to reduce foster care entries or improve placement stability
* Focus on preventing family separation over downstream interventions

3. MISSIONAL ALIGNMENT (20 points)
* Whole Family Approach: Solutions that strengthen entire families, not just children
* Church/Faith Engagement: Ability to mobilize or partner with faith communities
* Redemptive Orientation: Focus on restoration and healing, not just crisis response
* Collaborative Spirit: Commitment to field-building and sharing learnings
* Values-Driven Technology: Human dignity at center, technology as enabler not replacement

4. FEASIBILITY (20 points)
* Technical capacity and expertise on team
* Realistic timeline and milestones
* Clear implementation plan with defined phases
* Evidence of past project execution
* Understanding of data privacy and security requirements

5. SCALABILITY & SUSTAINABILITY (20 points)
* Path to serving 500+ families/youth within 2 years
* Open-source commitment and field-building potential (nonprofit track)
* Business model viability and scalability (social enterprise track)
* Revenue model or funding strategy post-grant
* Replication potential in other communities
* Partnership strategy for expansion

APPLICATION PROCESS

Step 1: Organizational Readiness Self-Assessment (Optional but Recommended)
Complete a brief 10-question online tool to determine organizational readiness. Takes 5-10 minutes.

Sample questions:
* Does your organization currently track cost per outcome for your programs?
* Do you have at least one staff member with basic technology proficiency?
* Can your organization commit to participating in quarterly learning sessions?
* Are you prepared to share learnings openly with the field?

Step 2: Full Application (Online Form)
1. Organization Information (10 minutes)
* Basic details, tax status, budget
* Team composition and technical expertise
* Current programs and populations served

2. Innovation Proposal (1,500 words max, ~45 minutes)
* Problem statement and solution overview
* Technology approach and innovation
* Target population and expected outcomes
* Implementation timeline

3. Budget Narrative (~30 minutes)
* Detailed budget breakdown for technology investment (40% for winners, 50% for runners-up — nonprofit track)
* Brief narrative on how flexible funding will strengthen organizational capacity
* Cost per outcome projections
* Sustainability plan

4. Supporting Materials
* 2-minute video pitch (required) — guidance provided
* Technical documentation (if applicable)
* Evidence of past work (optional)

Total estimated application time: 2-3 hours
Application features: save and return anytime, auto-save, templates provided (Theory of Change, Budget, Outcomes Framework)

PEER REVIEW PROCESS
Why Peer Review? Research shows that applicants find peer review one of the most valuable parts of competitive processes. It helps you:
* Understand how your proposal compares to others in the field
* Learn about potential partners and collaborators
* Receive practical, actionable feedback from practitioners
* Deepen your understanding of the evaluation criteria

How It Works:
* All eligible applicants will be invited to review 3-5 other applications
* Reviews are blind — you won't know who you're reviewing or who reviews you
* You'll use a simplified rubric (provided) to score and comment
* Takes approximately 2-3 hours total
* Your participation strengthens your own application and the entire field

Benefits:
* 93% of participants in similar processes rate peer review as valuable
* Organizations report forming lasting partnerships through this process
* Even non-winners gain insights that strengthen future funding applications

PROGRAM GUIDELINES

Use of Funds — Nonprofit Track
Flexible Portion (60% for winners, 50% for runners-up):
* Can be used for any organizational need
* Examples: staff salaries, rent, fundraising, capacity building, reserves
* No restrictions or additional reporting beyond standard grant reporting

Technology Innovation Portion (40% for winners, 50% for runners-up):
Allowable expenses include:
* Technology development and testing
* Staff time for implementation and training
* Professional development in AI/technology
* Data security and privacy infrastructure
* User research and testing
* Evaluation and measurement systems
* Direct service delivery costs related to innovation
* Equipment purchases (any amount — no cap)
* Technical assistance and consulting

No overhead cap. We recognize that effective work requires full organizational infrastructure.

Social Enterprise Track: Awards structured as investment or prize. Terms determined by Eagle Ventures Investment Committee.

Reporting Requirements
Light-touch reporting focused on learning, not compliance:
* Quarterly Check-ins: 30-minute Zoom calls with Flourish staff (conversational, not formal presentations)
* 6-Month Report: 3-page narrative + simple metrics dashboard
* 12-Month Report: 3-page narrative + metrics dashboard + 1 success story
* Final Report (18 months): 5-page narrative + final metrics + reflections on what worked/what didn't

Key metrics we track:
* Cost per outcome (baseline vs. current)
* Number of families/youth served
* Technology implementation milestones
* Open-source releases and adoption by others (nonprofit track)
* Partnership formation

Philosophy: We want to learn WITH you, not monitor you. Reports should be honest about challenges and pivots, not just highlight successes.

Open Source Commitment (Nonprofit Track Only)
Per Google.org requirements, nonprofit track grantees must:
* Release any code under Apache 2.0 license within 3 months of creation
* Share other materials under Creative Commons CC-BY-4.0
* Make innovations searchable and accessible to others
* No patents on grant-funded innovations
* Participate in one public webinar to share learnings

This ensures the entire field benefits from our collective investments.

Social Enterprise Track: No open-source IP requirements. No IP restrictions.

RISK MITIGATION
Risk: Limited technical capacity in small organizations
Mitigation: Provide technical assistance through Project Evident; build learning community; flexible funding for capacity building

Risk: Data privacy concerns with vulnerable populations
Mitigation: Require privacy plan in application; provide templates and resources; connect with pro bono legal support

Risk: Sustainability challenges post-grant
Mitigation: Require sustainability plan; 60/50% flexible funding strengthens organizational foundation; provide fundraising support through network; Eagle Ventures provides follow-on investment pathway for social enterprises

Risk: Limited applications or lack of diversity
Mitigation: Extended outreach through both Flourish Fund and Eagle Ventures networks; simplified application process; organizational readiness tool; targeted recruitment

Risk: Small team capacity for intensive support
Mitigation: Leverage peer learning model; use technology for efficiency (video submissions, online forms); focus support on finalists; build alumni network for peer support

Risk: Cross-sector coordination challenges (nonprofit/for-profit tracks)
Mitigation: Clear decision rights documented; separate operational platforms; shared judges build coherence; joint branding requires mutual approval

CONCLUSION
The Flourish Fund Innovation Challenge represents a first-of-its-kind opportunity to bring nonprofits and social enterprises together under one umbrella to solve foster care's most pressing challenges. By combining Flourish Fund's grant-making expertise with Eagle Ventures' investment capability, we create multiple pathways for innovators to receive funding and support.

Incorporating best practices — including peer review, comprehensive feedback, an innovators network, flexible funding, and a live pitch event at Praxis Summit — we create value for all participants while identifying high-potential innovations across sectors.

This approach allows Flourish Fund and its partners to efficiently identify innovations while building a cross-sector community committed to transforming foster care through technology, faith, and collaboration.
