I'm heading back to everyday life with those moments tucked in my pocket. They helped so much, and I'm holding onto that.

## Sacred Mantra Reflections (from Membership Applications)

### "Leave Anyway"

"With a family of five, the hardest part of camping is never the camping itself – it's just getting out the door. Between everyone's schedules, the endless packing lists, and the mental checklist of things left undone, it always feels like I'm behind. 'Motion creates energy' reminds me that when we finally pull away from the driveway everything starts to fall into place. It's never easy to leave but every time we do I'm reminded how much we really needed it."
– Aftan Miller

"That was the mantra that my family and I used when we decided to go and live in an RV for 14 months. We chose to trust that God would order our footsteps and He did. We left safety and control, stepped out into nature, and realized that everything that we were looking for was always surrounding us."
– Wale Forrester

"Our family tends to overthink all of the potential obstacles and challenges that can get in the way of going camping – scheduling, the right weather conditions, having time to prep and pack supplies. We have skipped several opportunities in the past. Having the support of the Outdoorithm Collective to move past that and 'Leave Anyway' resonates with what I think we need to be able to spend more time outdoors and camping."
– Jonathan Brack

"I love this, I wish I'd do this more… so many times I didn't feel ready or that I had all I needed. I have had many missed opportunities over the years and I'd like to start new with accepting opportunities to be outside when they come to me."
– Janel Holloway-Lee

"Just booked a trip to Spain with my two best friends on a whim, no research and just decided to figure it out when we got there. We had an incredible time just exploring, meeting locals for advice and camaraderie and it enhanced our experience in ways doing hours of research never would have."
– Michael Munoz

"The prep work is daunting for camping and has kept us home in the past but having a community waiting is motivating, knowing that all of us had to overcome that barrier to get out there."

– Valencia Miller

"Most days we're juggling kids, work, and a very curious puppy – nothing ever lines up perfectly. Nerves around wanting to give the kids a good experience or worrying we'd leave something needed limited our ability to get out there. Last summer, we just went for it – taking our 4 and 5 year old out on Memorial Day with no reservation, found an abandoned equestrian horse camp 15 yards off a river by Wrights Lake and enjoyed every second."
– Josh Pollack

"Being outdoors teaches us to roll with the punches and make do with the situation. There is no bail out button; we need to accept situations and work through challenges to get home."

"'Camp as it comes' reminds me of our national road trips, and having to adjust at every new spot. Inevitably, we would meet someone new, eat somewhere exciting, and have a memory that would last a lifetime."
