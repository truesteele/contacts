## Bottom Line: Raise the Goal from $50K to $100K

The original campaign targets $50K. The network data and recent giving signals say you can do more — but not as much as the AI-scored "ask readiness" suggests on paper. The gap between *capacity* and *actual giving behavior* is real: 2025 Stripe data shows 75 donors, $30K total, median gift of $100, and only one gift above $2,000. ## Why $50K Is Still Too Low

Even grounded in the real data, $50K underestimates what this campaign can do. Here's why:

### 1. The year-end was not a campaign

A non-ask that raised $15K suggests a real campaign with specific dollar amounts, a match, and urgency will raise substantially more. Research shows focused campaigns with matching gifts outperform soft asks by 2-4x. That puts the realistic floor at $30K-$60K from the email alone.

### 2. Pre-campaign personal outreach changes the math

The original plan calls for 10-15 inner circle contacts. These are direct, personal emails and texts to people like Brigitte Hoyer Gosselink, Tyler Scriven, Patrick Dickinson, Erin Teague — people who *can* give $5K-$25K but won't unless personally invited. Even 8-10 commitments at $2.5K-$5K average adds $20K-$50K that the broader email campaign will never generate. And some of these conversations will naturally evolve into phone calls when the donor wants to go deeper.

### 3. The $20K match is a force multiplier

The $20K match doesn't just add $20K — it changes the psychology of every gift. Research shows matching increases average donation by 51% and participation by 22%. On a $50K goal, the match alone gets you to $70K+ before the third email.

### 4. The endowed progress problem

With the $20K match + $15K-$25K in pre-commitments, a $50K campaign launches at 70-90% funded. The goal gradient effect works against you — donors feel the campaign is done. A $100K goal with $35K-$45K on day one launches at 35-45% — perfect position for the endowed progress effect.

## Why $100K, Not $150K

The first version of this analysis recommended $150K based on AI-scored ask readiness. That was the *theoretical ceiling* based on network capacity. Here's why $100K is the right number for this campaign:

### 1. The actual giving data demands humility

75 donors gave $30K in a full year. The median gift was $100. Jumping from that to a $150K campaign goal in 3 weeks requires an unrealistic number of conversions at unrealistic gift sizes. $100K requires a realistic stretch: tripling the donor count AND tripling the average gift through direct asks and the match.

### 2. This is OC's first real campaign

The year-end email was not a campaign. This will be the first time Justin directly asks his broader network for money with specific amounts and a clear goal. First campaigns should be achievable and buildable. Exceeding a $100K goal creates a better story and more confidence than falling short of $150K.

### 3. Justin's personal bandwidth is the bottleneck

The larger gifts come from direct personal outreach — emails and texts to the inner circle, with phone calls happening when the recipient wants to talk further. Justin can realistically send 15-25 personal messages in the pre-campaign window and follow up on conversations that develop. That's the constraint — not network capacity but founder time. $100K is sized to what personal outreach to 15-25 people + a 3-email campaign can deliver.

### 4. Your own principles say: don't over-optimize

"The moment an email starts to feel 'crafted' — strategic quote placement, hero framing, pronoun ratios — it stops feeling human." A $150K goal requires maximizing every contact. A $100K goal lets you be warm, genuine, and still succeed.

## Campaign Math: How $100K Happens

### Phase 1: Pre-Campaign Personal Outreach — Target $25K-$40K

**15-25 personal emails/texts to the inner circle.** These are people who:
- Are scored "ready_now" with suggested asks of $5K+
- Have direct personal relationship with Justin
- Have *capacity* (Google directors, VPs, founders, family office managers)
- But will NOT give without a direct, personal invitation

**The approach:** A personal email or text — not a pitch, not a phone call. Something like: "Hey — OC is doing 10 trips this season. I'm about to ask my broader network to help fund them. Before I do, I wanted to reach out to you personally. Would love to count you in. If you want to talk about it, I'm around."

People who want to commit will reply. People who want to discuss will ask for a call. Those calls — initiated by the donor — will be far more productive than an unsolicited ask.

**Realistic expectations:**
- 15-25 personal messages sent
- 8-15 responses (50-60% for close friends)
- 5-10 commitments, plus 3-5 conversations that lead to larger gifts
- Average commitment: $2.5K-$5K
- Range: $1K to $10K+ (a few will go higher, many at $2.5K-$5K)
- **Phase 1 target: $25K-$40K**

*This is where the campaign is won or lost.* The broader email campaign is the second act, not the main event.

## The Revised Framing

### Keep the math simple and trip-focused

The original framing is strong: "10 trips. $10K each. $100K total." Don't blow it up to $250K. The campaign story is:

**"We need $100K to fund every trip this season. $40K is already committed from early supporters. $60K to go — and a friend is matching the first $20K dollar-for-dollar."**

This is clean:
- Launches at 40% funded (endowed progress effect)
- $60K gap feels achievable (goal gradient)
- Match creates urgency ("first $20K")
- Trip math is concrete

### Raise the anchor points

Original anchors: $1,000 / $2,500 / $5,000
**Revised anchors: $1,000 / $2,500 / $5,000** — keep these.

The 2025 data shows the $5K anchor was aspirational and only one person hit it. But $1K/$2.5K/$5K is the right range for this network when directly asked. The $1K floor is important — it's accessible for the Google/HBS crowd and it "sends two families camping."

For personal outreach to the inner circle, anchor at $2.5K/$5K/$10K. Different audience, different ask. Let the conversation find the right level — don't lead with the highest number.

### The $20K match positioning

"A friend has committed to matching the first $20,000 in donations dollar-for-dollar. Your $2,500 becomes $5,000 — half a trip funded."

**Consider asking the match donor to increase to $25K-$30K.** At $20K, the match will be claimed quickly (4-8 donors at $2.5K). A larger match extends the urgency window through the full campaign.

## What the Ask Readiness Data Is Good For

The AI ask readiness scores are NOT predictions of what people will give. They're a **prioritization tool** for who to reach out to, how to personalize the approach, and who gets personal outreach vs. the broader email campaign. Use them for:

1. **Personal outreach list**: Sort ready_now by score, prioritize those with suggested asks of $5K+. The top 20 contacts (score 88+) include founders, VPs, and family office managers who can give at scale — but only when personally invited.

2. **Email segmentation**: Ready_now contacts get Email 1 first. Cultivate_first at 75+ get it simultaneously or on a 1-day delay. Lower-scored contacts get Email 2 and 3 only.

3. **Personalization angles**: Each contact's `personalization_angle` and `receiver_frame` fields tell you how to frame the invitation in a way that connects to *their* values. Use these for the personal outreach messages especially.

4. **Approach recommendations**: The `recommended_approach` field (in_person, personal_email, linkedin, text_message) tells you the optimal channel. For contacts marked "in_person," send a personal email/text that opens the door to meeting up. The call or meeting happens when they respond and want it.

### The core truth

This network has the capacity to give $150K+ in a single campaign. The 2025 data proves it — someone gave $100K. But that capacity is only unlocked through direct, personal, one-to-one engagement. The email campaign creates the container, the match creates urgency, and the social proof creates momentum. But the personal outreach — and the conversations that follow when people respond — is where the larger gifts come from.

**Phone calls are earned, not initiated.** The personal email or text creates the invitation. People who want to engage deeper will reply and ask for a call. Those donor-initiated conversations will be far more productive than unsolicited asks. Research backs this: phone is most effective for thank-yous and retention, not solicitation. Even with warm contacts, an unexpected call asking for money creates discomfort.

**$100K is the right goal** because it's achievable with personal outreach to 15-25 inner circle contacts and a strong 3-email campaign, it's ambitious enough to stretch beyond what happened organically in 2025, and it positions OC as serious without overreaching in its first real campaign. Hit $100K, celebrate, and raise the bar to $150K in 2027 with a proven playbook and a warm donor base.
