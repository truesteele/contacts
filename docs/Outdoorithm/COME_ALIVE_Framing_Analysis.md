## The Frame: COME ALIVE

**Outdoorithm Collective helps families come alive - together, in nature.**

Not "outdoor equity." Not "social capital." Not "civic infrastructure." Those are the mechanisms. The story is simpler and more primal:

**We've forgotten how to be alive. And nature + community is how we remember.**

Here's why this frame is the one:

### 1. It's visceral and intuitive

Every single person you're trying to reach - donor, participant, partner - knows the feeling of being numbed. Of scrolling at 11pm and feeling hollow. Of realizing they haven't had a real conversation in weeks. Of watching their kid disappear into a screen. You don't have to explain the problem. They feel it in their chest.

And every single person has also had that moment - on a hike, around a fire, watching a sunset - where something *woke up*. Where the world got sharp and vivid again. Where they felt present. Where they felt *alive*.

"Come alive" connects those two universal experiences: the numbness and the awakening. No policy expertise required.

### 2. It meets the cultural moment perfectly

The timing is extraordinary. Consider what's converging right now:

- **The Surgeon General's loneliness epidemic** - isolation literally kills like 15 cigarettes a day. The US dropped to 24th in the World Happiness Report.
- **The Over-Optimization Backlash** - The Global Wellness Summit's #1 trend for 2026 is the revolt against measurement-heavy, tech-mediated, individual wellness. People are exhausted by biohacking and self-tracking. They want to *feel*, not optimize.
- **Friction-maxxing** - The Guardian and The Cut are naming what millions feel: convenience is making us less capable, less resilient, less human. People are *choosing* inconvenience as liberation.
- **Jean Case** - A woman who helped build the internet is now begging people to log off. "Strolling over scrolling." Nature as "the wellness program hiding in plain sight."
- **Baratunde Thurston** - One of the most important cultural voices of the moment is writing poetry about moving from "intelligence of an artificial nature" back to "the intelligence that lives within all nature." His closing line: *"To live well collectively in the future, in the cloud, we must also plant our feet firmly in the now, on the ground."*
- **AI anxiety** - As AI automates everything, the question *"What does it mean to be human?"* is no longer philosophical. It's existential. And the answer isn't on a screen.

Every single one of these cultural currents flows directly into what OC already does. You're not chasing a trend. **The culture is arriving at your doorstep.**

### 4. It's asset-framed, not deficit-framed

"Ending isolation" is important but deficit-framed. It tells donors what you're fighting *against*. "Come alive" tells them what you're building *toward*. In movement-building, aspiration always beats desperation. People don't march against darkness. They march toward light.

### How the Six Dimensions Reinforce Each Other

The six dimensions don't operate in isolation. They create a reinforcing cycle where each one deepens the others:

**Land feeds Rest.** Fresh air, natural soundscapes (ocean, creek, wind in trees), and circadian reset from natural light cycles create the conditions for the deepest rest people have experienced in years.

**Grit feeds Community.** The stuck van. The rainstorm. The raccoon. Shared adversity creates bonds that shared comfort never could. The community forms *because* of the grit moments, not despite them.

**Community feeds Land.** For families carrying generational exclusion from nature, the land connection requires feeling safe - and safety comes from community. Carl can feel comfortable in the woods *because* he's surrounded by people who see and celebrate him.

**Rest feeds everything.** When you're genuinely rested - not just "slept 7 hours" but deeply restored - you're more present for community, more open to the land, more resilient when grit is required, more capable of movement, more grateful for nourishment.
