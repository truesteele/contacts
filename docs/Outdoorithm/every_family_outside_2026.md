# **Pre-Campaign Work (Feb 13 – Feb 25\)**

This is the most important phase. Research is clear: campaigns that launch with 50%+ progress convert dramatically better. The endowed progress effect means every subsequent donor feels like they’re “helping finish” rather than “starting from scratch.”

## **1\. Secure a Matching Challenge ($5K–$10K)**

Call 3–5 most likely candidates. A match doesn’t need to be complicated: “Someone in my network has committed to matching the first $5,000 in donations dollar-for-dollar” is one of the most powerful lines you can put in an email. If you can’t get one, the campaign still works — but this single tactic increases average donations by 51% according to research.

## **2\. Make 10–15 Phone Calls to the Inner Circle**

These are the people who would give $2,500–$5,000 — closest friends from HBS, Google, etc. The goal isn’t to close them on the phone. It’s to tell them the campaign is coming and that you’d love to count them in. Many will commit right there.

**Goal: $15K–$25K in commitments before the first email goes out.** This means the campaign launches showing real momentum.

## **3\. Get Permission for Social Proof**

You don’t need to name names in the email. But being able to say “several friends from Google and Harvard have already committed” or “15 people have backed the season so far” is powerful. Ask early commitments if you can reference them (even anonymously by affiliation).

## **4\. Employer Matching Reminder**

Many contacts work at companies with matching programs (Google/Benevity, Salesforce, Microsoft, Meta, etc.). A $2,500 personal gift becomes $5,000 with a match. Mention this naturally — it’s free money.

# **Why These Strategic Choices**

## **Why $50K Is the Right Goal**

* With 100–250 contacts and an expected 5–10% conversion rate, expect 10–25 donors

* Average gift of $1,500–$2,500 from this audience \= $15K–$62K range

* $50K is achievable and exceeding it feels great (endowed progress \+ celebration)

* The remaining \~$15K gap is covered by the expected Parks California grant and other fundraising

## **Why the 3-Week Window Works**

* Joshua Tree (March 30\) provides genuine urgency — the season is actually starting

* 3 weeks is long enough to process and act, short enough to maintain urgency

* Research shows 7–14 days is optimal, but the natural window is \~3 weeks

## **Why Plain Text from Your Personal Email**

The fundraising principles explicitly say “personal emails, not marketing pitches.” The Mattias exchange proves this works — $2,500 \+ match from a personal email thread. This audience gets designed nonprofit emails all the time. A personal note stands out. Subject lines from a personal email get higher open rates than branded campaigns.

## **What We Should NOT Do**

Don’t manufacture urgency — the season genuinely starts March 30\. Don’t let donors “own” trips. Don’t over-optimize — warm and slightly imperfect beats polished and hollow. Don’t use nonprofit jargon. Don’t compromise trip experiences for fundraising.

# **Research-Backed Principles**

These principles informed every decision in this campaign design:

### **Endowed Progress Effect**

People exert more effort as they approach a goal. Never launch a campaign at 0%. By showing $35K already raised, every donor feels like they’re helping finish rather than starting from scratch.

### **Identifiable Victim Effect**

Donors give more than twice as much when presented with an individual story vs. statistics. Carl’s quote is more powerful than “we serve 100+ families.” Lead with the person, not the data.

### **Social Proof**

When donors see others contributing, they give more. Mentioning Harvard/Google affiliations creates powerful normative influence among peers.

### **Specificity Over Vagueness**

“$1,000 sends two families to the campfire” beats “your gift makes a difference.” Clear unit economics build confidence and remove ambiguity.

### **Matching Gifts**

Mentioning matching gifts increases average donation by 51%. The probability of any donation increases 22% with matching. A simple 1:1 match is just as effective as 2:1 or 3:1.

### **Goal Gradient Effect**

People exert more effort as they approach a goal — “running faster when you see the finish line.” Each progress update in Emails 2 and 3 triggers this effect.

### **Genuine Urgency & Donor-Centric Language**

Urgency works when it’s real. “Season starts March 30” is not manufactured. “If you want in” positions donors as joining something, not saving something.
