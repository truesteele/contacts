The Steeles camp year-round, but the data reveals clear seasonal rhythms.

**Year-round commitment.** Every season gets at least 20% of the family's camping nights. Winter and fall are nearly equal — the Steeles don't wait for warm weather to pitch a tent.

**July is king:** More camping nights in July than any other month by a wide margin. Summer is prime time, but the family doesn't hibernate when the temperatures drop.

## Booking Persistence

The reservation data reveals something about the Steele family that casual campers wouldn't understand: **the hustle of getting a campsite in California.**

| Metric | Count |
|--------|:-----:|
| Total reservations booked | 381 |
| Reservations that became trips | 136 |
| Cancelled reservations | 233 |
| **Cancellation rate** | **61%** |

A 61% cancellation rate isn't indecisiveness — it's strategy. California's most popular campgrounds (Pfeiffer Big Sur, Kirk Creek, Yosemite) sell out in minutes. Experienced campers book multiple options when windows open, then consolidate to the best site as plans firm up. The data proves it:

| Campground | Bookings | Completed | Cancel Rate |
|-----------|:--------:|:---------:|:-----------:|
| Pfeiffer Big Sur | 25 | 6 | 76% |
| New Brighton | 24 | 9 | 63% |
| Kirk Creek | 16 | 2 | 88% |
| Pinnacles | 11 | 3 | 73% |
| Lake Alpine | 10 | 5 | 50% |

Kirk Creek and Pfeiffer Big Sur have 76%+ cancellation rates — not because the family is flaky, but because those campgrounds are among the most competitive in the entire state park system.
