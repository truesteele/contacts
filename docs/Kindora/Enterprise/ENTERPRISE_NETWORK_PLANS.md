# Kindora Enterprise Network Plans

**Version:** 2.1
**Last Updated:** March 6, 2026
**Status:** Active

---

## Overview

Enterprise Network Plans allow a single entity (foundation, nonprofit network, accelerator, or capacity builder) to purchase Kindora licenses for multiple organizations in its network. The sponsor buys a block of Individual-tier licenses at a volume discount, receives redemption codes to distribute, and gets enablement + a sponsor admin dashboard.

**Contract structure:** Order Form (deal-specific terms) + Standard Terms (legal backbone at kindora.co/terms/enterprise). See `STANDARD_TERMS.md` and `ORDER_FORM_TEMPLATE.md`.

---

## Anchor Plan

All enterprise licenses are based on the **Individual plan ($49/mo)**, which includes:
- 10 Intel Briefs/month
- 500 Kira Credits/month
- 3 team seats + 3 guest seats per org
- 5 programs per org
- Pipeline + Saved Searches
- Advanced Exports
- 60-day credit/brief rollover

## Payment Options

Every enterprise deal offers the sponsor a choice of two billing structures:

| | Option A: Full Prepay | Option B: Split Billing |
|---|---|---|
| **What's paid upfront** | 100% of target org count | 2/3 of target org count |
| **Variable billing** | None | 1/3 billed quarterly as orgs activate |
| **Discount** | Higher (see tier table) | Lower (see tier table) |
| **Best for** | Sponsors confident in adoption | Sponsors wanting flexibility |
| **Invoice type** | Single annual invoice | Annual invoice + quarterly variable invoices |

### Why two options
- Sponsors self-select based on their budget cycle and adoption confidence
- Full prepay rewards commitment with a better rate and gets Kindora more cash upfront
- Split billing reduces sponsor risk and aligns cost with actual adoption
- The 5% gap between options is enough to reward commitment without penalizing flexibility

| Option | Per-Org/Mo | Annual (30 orgs) |
|--------|-----------|-----------------|
| **A: Full Prepay** | $39/mo | $14,040 |

- Do not go below $30/org/mo at any tier
- Do not negotiate custom pricing below 50 orgs — use published tiers
- Custom pricing (100+) requires a defined adoption plan and shared success metrics
- Multi-year deals (2 yr): additional 10% discount on either option

## Billing Model

### Option A: Full Prepay
- Single annual invoice for all target orgs, paid upfront at contract signing
- Net-30 payment terms
- One line item: "Kindora Enterprise Network Plan — [N] org licenses × $[rate]/mo × 12 months"

### Option B: Split Billing (2/3 Fixed + 1/3 Variable)

| Component | Proportion | When Billed | How Billed |
|-----------|------------|-------------|------------|
| **Fixed commitment** | 2/3 of target org count | At contract signing | Annual invoice, net-30 |
| **Variable band** | Remaining 1/3 of target | As orgs activate | Quarterly invoice, net-30 |
| **Overage** | Above target count | As orgs activate | Quarterly invoice at list rate ($49/mo) |

**Quarterly variable invoicing:**
- Invoiced at end of each calendar quarter based on actual code redemptions
- Line item: "Additional org activations — [N] orgs × $[rate]/mo × [months active in quarter]"
- Pro-rated for mid-quarter activations (billed from activation date to quarter end)
- Sponsor receives activation summary with each quarterly invoice (aggregate counts only)

**Why quarterly invoicing (not credit card):**
- Nonprofits and foundations run purchases through finance/procurement — surprise credit card charges create friction
- Quarterly cadence balances transparency with low admin overhead
- Gives the sponsor a natural checkpoint to review adoption
- Invoices can reference a PO number if the sponsor requires it

### Ramp schedule (50+ org deals, Option B only)

| Period | Fixed Billing | Notes |
|--------|---------------|-------|
| Months 1–3 | 50% of fixed commitment | Onboarding & code distribution |
| Months 4–12 | 100% of fixed commitment | Full billing |
| Year 2 | True-up to actual usage | Adjust fixed count based on real adoption |

## License Distribution: Redemption Codes

Sponsors receive **redemption codes** to distribute to their member organizations.

### Code mechanics

- **One code per enterprise contract** (e.g., `NETWORK-2026`)
- **Redemption cap** matching total target count
- **Expiration window:** 90–180 days from contract start
- **Self-serve activation:** Org signs up at kindora.co, enters code during registration, Individual plan activates automatically — no credit card required
- **Codes are not transferable** beyond the cap and expiration

### Why codes (not alternatives)

| Method | Why it doesn't work for nonprofit networks |
|--------|---------------------------------------------|
| Domain verification | Member orgs all have different email domains |
| Invite-by-email | Friction; sponsor must collect every org's email |
| Manual provisioning | Doesn't scale; requires Kindora staff per org |
| **Redemption codes** | **Self-serve, simple, trackable, cap-enforced** |

### What the sponsor tells member orgs

> "Sign up at kindora.co and enter code **[CODE]** during registration to activate your sponsored Individual plan."

## Sponsor Admin Dashboard

Enterprise sponsors get a dashboard showing:

- **Redemption status:** Codes redeemed vs. remaining (count only)
- **Activation metrics:** Active orgs, briefs generated, credits used (aggregate)
- **Adoption health:** Orgs that signed up but haven't used the platform in 30+ days
- **Privacy boundary:** Sponsors do NOT see individual org pipelines, searches, funder strategy, or grant applications

## Renewal Terms

### True-up / true-down at renewal

- **Grew:** Activated 35 of 30 target → renew at new target of 35
- **Met target:** Activated 28 of 30 → renew at target of 28 (no penalty for 2 unused)
- **Underperformed:** Activated only 15 → reduce commitment or add enablement support
- **Maximum true-down without tier renegotiation:** 20% of prior target

### Renewal pricing

- Renewal at same tier rate unless org count drops below tier threshold
- No automatic price increases in Year 2 (revisit at Year 3)
- Multi-year option: 2-year commitment earns additional 10% discount

## Enterprise Buyer Types

### 1. Nonprofit Networks / Capacity Builders
- **Wedge:** "Standardize grant prospecting across members; cut research time"
- **Code distribution:** Network sends code in member newsletter or cohort kickoff

### 2. Foundations / Funders (Grantee-Sponsored)
- **Wedge:** "Capacity-building that helps grantees diversify revenue, with privacy guardrails"
- **Critical:** Grantees own their accounts; funder does NOT see pipelines or strategy

### 3. Accelerators / Fellowships
- **Wedge:** "Equip fellows with professional grant intelligence as a program benefit"
- **Code distribution:** Cohort manager distributes code at program kickoff

### 4. Consultant Networks (at Scale)
- **Wedge:** "Higher throughput per grant writer + consistent quality across clients"
- **Note:** For individual consultants (≤10 clients), the Consultant plan ($399/mo) is better
- **Enterprise applies when:** 15+ independent consultants or client orgs

## Anchor Pricing Strategy

The founding enterprise partner (Camelback Ventures) is priced at 15% — deliberately at the top of the market. All published tiers offer better rates than the anchor:

| Scenario | Option A | Option B | Framing |
|----------|----------|----------|---------|
| Founding partner (Camelback) | — | 15% | "Founding Enterprise Partner rate" |
| Starter Network (5–14) | 10% | 6% | Published tier |
| Growth Network (15–49) | 20% | 15% | "Better than our founding partner" |
| Impact Network (50–99) | 30% | 24% | "Deepest published tier" |
| Enterprise (100+) | Custom | Custom | Camelback as ceiling reference |

---

## Pricing Principles

1. **Discounts are earned, not given** — tied to commitment size, term, and adoption expectations
2. **Discounts are bounded** — published tiers prevent ad-hoc negotiation erosion
3. **Two options, not negotiation** — sponsors choose between full prepay and split billing; don't create custom hybrid structures
4. **Margins are protected** — never go below $30/org/mo
5. **Mission alignment matters** — for a PBC, the split billing model prevents charging for unused licenses while maintaining revenue predictability

---

## Anti-Patterns (What NOT to Do)

- **Don't offer per-org monthly billing** for enterprise
- **Don't go below $30/org/mo** at any tier
- **Don't make codes transferable** without caps
- **Don't negotiate custom pricing below 50 orgs**
- **Don't give the sponsor visibility into individual org data**
- **Don't discount more than 30%** without multi-year + 50+ orgs
- **Don't let true-down exceed 20%** at renewal without renegotiating tier
- **Don't bill the variable portion via credit card** — quarterly invoicing only
- **Don't create a third hybrid billing option** — two choices is enough
