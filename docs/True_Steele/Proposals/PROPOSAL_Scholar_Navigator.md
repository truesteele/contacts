Mo-saIQ is building something that doesn't exist: a personalized guidance system for high-achieving Black and brown students navigating competitive STEM and entrepreneurship pathways. You have the vision, the curriculum, the scholars, and one year of proven programming. What you don't have is the technology platform that makes this scalable and persistent.

Your scholars are falling through the gaps between these systems.

The iQNNECTED Scholar Navigator closes that gap. Waze for a scholar's STEM journey, with their identity as a strength.

---

## What We'll Build Together

The Scholar Navigator is an AI-powered platform that takes a scholar from "who am I?" to "here's my next step" and keeps them on track from 9th grade through college entry.

**The core experience:**

1. **Know Yourself.** A scenario-based assessment (inspired by 16 Personalities, not a form) that captures who a scholar is authentically: interests, strengths, goals, cultural identity, and the environments where they thrive.

2. **Chart Your Path.** AI generates a personalized roadmap: recommended STEM/entrepreneurship pathways, academic prep needed, aligned opportunities, and grade-specific milestones. The roadmap adapts as interests evolve.

3. **Navigate.** An interactive dashboard with milestone tracking, affirmations, deadline nudges, and pressure-test prompts ("Is this what you really want?"). Scholars drive their own destination.

4. **Manage.** An admin dashboard so you can see every scholar's progress, manage content, and track program health. No more spreadsheets.

## What Comes Next (Phase 2 and Beyond)

Phase 1 builds the core loop: assessment, roadmap, milestones. But I heard your full vision, and the platform is architected to grow into it. Here's what Phase 2 looks like:

- **Social capital network.** Scholar-to-scholar connections, peer discovery by pathway, milestone sharing. "Does anybody in my network know about this?" You described this as central to the experience, and I agree. It's the first priority after the core product is proven.
- **AI mentor chatbot.** A conversational interface grounded in Mo-saIQ's voice and scholar stories. Scholars can ask questions, pressure-test decisions, and get encouragement from something that feels like talking to someone who gets it.
- **Live session integration.** Replace the Zoom/Google Classroom setup with in-platform scheduling and access for your virtual sessions, boot camps, and college prep.
- **Advisor and caregiver portals.** Dr. Roystone sees assigned scholars and adds notes. Guardians get read-only progress views.
- **Notification system, enhanced analytics, pulse check surveys.**

Phase 3 adds the social media engagement layer (quick reactions, visual feed, peer celebrations), mentorship matching, gamification, multilingual support, and in-platform applications to replace Wufoo.

**Not in scope for any phase:** native mobile app (the web app works on mobile; a native app is a separate project), gamified math/science practice modules (separate product), ongoing opportunity database maintenance post-handoff.
