## Part 2: Voice Recordings

Three short recordings that give me the context I can't get from your data. Record on your phone (voice memo is perfect), Zoom, or Otter. Send to justin@truesteele.com.

**Total time: 30-45 minutes across 3 recordings.**

> **Start each recording with:** your name, date, and the module name.
>
> **Consent statement (read once at the start of your first recording):**
> *"This recording is for the True Steele fundraising intelligence sprint. It will be transcribed and used to build our donor analysis and funder strategy. Only the project team will access it. We may use AI to help transcribe."*

---

### Recording 1 — Laura King
**SFEF Fundraising Landscape | 12-15 min**

The goal here is to give me the insider view of your donor base and fundraising operation that doesn't show up in a CRM export.

1. Walk me through your current individual giving program. How do you cultivate and solicit major gifts today? What's working and what's not?

2. Who are your top 5-10 most important donor relationships right now? For each, give me a sentence on who they are, how they're connected to SFEF, and where the relationship stands.

3. Are there donors or prospects you know you should be cultivating but haven't had the bandwidth to pursue? Who are they and what's the opportunity?

4. What fundraising channels have you tried (events, direct mail, online, peer-to-peer, planned giving)? Which ones have worked best for SFEF specifically?

5. What does your board's role in fundraising look like today? Do board members give, solicit, open doors, or is that an area for growth?

6. Are there any sensitive dynamics I should know about — lapsed major donors, tricky relationships, political considerations, or donor fatigue?

---

### Recording 2 — Terrence Riley
**SFLC Funding Story | 10-12 min**

I need to understand the Literacy Coalition's funding landscape deeply enough to write compelling funder outreach.

1. Give me the 2-minute pitch for the SF Literacy Coalition. If you were in an elevator with a program officer at a major education foundation, what would you say?

2. What outcomes can you point to? Student numbers served, literacy gains, program completion rates, any data points that demonstrate impact.

3. Walk me through your current funding mix. What percentage comes from government, foundations, corporate, individual donors, earned revenue? Where are the gaps?

4. Which funders have said yes in the past, and why do you think they said yes? Which funders have said no, and what was their reasoning?

5. What makes SFLC different from other literacy programs in the Bay Area? What's the unique angle a funder couldn't find elsewhere?

6. If you could secure funding from any 3 foundations tomorrow, which would they be and why?

---

### Recording 3 — Ann Levy Walden
**Operations & Donor Engagement | 8-10 min**

I need to understand how the development operation actually runs day-to-day.

1. Walk me through what happens when a new donor makes their first gift. What's the acknowledgment process? How do they get folded into your communications?

2. What does your event pipeline look like? What events do you host or attend, and how do those connect to donor cultivation?

3. How do you currently track donor interactions — calls, meetings, emails, event attendance? Is that in your CRM, a spreadsheet, someone's head, or a mix?

4. What's the biggest operational bottleneck in your development work right now?

5. Are there volunteers, committee members, or community champions who are deeply connected to SFEF but aren't major donors? Who should I know about?
