## Part 2: Key Questions

Written responses are perfect for these. A few sentences to a paragraph each. If it's easier to record a voice memo, send to justin@truesteele.com and I'll transcribe.

### For Cesar — Strategy & Impact (15-20 min)

1. **Government partnerships:** Walk me through your most important government partnerships. Who are they, what's the relationship, and what's the evidence of buy-in? Which government partner(s) would provide a letter of support?

2. **AI use case prioritization:** Between the two use cases (agentic application review vs. automated benefits counseling), which do you think is stronger for this application? Why? Is one further along than the other?

3. **Scale and reach:** If you had $1-3M and 36 months, how big could this get? What's realistic? How many government partners could you expand to? What's the potential member reach?

4. **What makes UpTogether different?** If you're in front of a Google.org evaluator and they ask "why should we fund you over 100 other applicants?" — what's your answer?

5. **Sensitive dynamics:** Anything I should know about — internal politics, board considerations, government partner sensitivities, past application failures, competitive dynamics?

### For Rachel — Technology & Feasibility (10-15 min)

1. **Tech stack and readiness:** How would you describe UpTogether's current technical maturity? What's the engineering team size? What's realistic to build in 36 months?

2. **AI feasibility:** For each use case, what's the honest assessment of technical feasibility? What are the biggest technical risks? What data do you have access to that would make AI work well?

3. **Data infrastructure:** What member data do you collect? How clean is it? Are there privacy or consent considerations I should know about for the ethics section?

4. **Build vs. buy:** Is the expectation to build AI capabilities in-house, use third-party AI services (like Google Cloud AI), or some combination?

### For Ivanna — Operations & Coordination (5-10 min)

1. **Primary point of contact for the application:** Who should be listed? Name, title, work email, timezone.

2. **Second point of contact:** Same info.

3. **Anything I'm missing?** As someone close to the day-to-day, are there operational realities or context I should factor in?
