## What This Is

This document contains guided prompts for short voice recordings that will become the foundation of your Scholar Navigator product requirements document (PRD). You talk, we transcribe, we build the spec.

We've done deep research on Mo-Saiq's programs, public materials, and current digital footprint. These prompts are designed to fill the gaps that only you can answer — we won't waste your time asking things we already know.

**Total time ask: 45–70 minutes across 5 recordings.**

## How to Record

- Record each module as a separate voice memo (phone, Zoom, Otter, etc.).
- Target 8–15 minutes per module. Stop early if you've covered the prompts.
- Start every recording with: name, role, date, module name.
- Use real examples. If possible, tell the story of a specific scholar or moment from the Empowerment Program.
- Please anonymize sensitive details (names, locations, or anything identifying a minor).

## Nikole Collins-Puri Modules (CEO / Product Owner)

Complete these 5 modules to generate a PRD-quality understanding of the Scholar Navigator.

---

### NC1 — Vision, Success, Boundaries
**Duration:** 8–12 min | **Goal:** Define what "winning" looks like and what the Scholar Navigator must respect.

**Prompts:**

1. In one sentence: what is the Scholar Navigator for?
   - *You've described it as "a school navigator in your pocket that affirms who you are." How would you explain it to a scholar's parent or guardian?*

2. You mentioned Project Foster and Reach Pathways as inspiration — what do those get right, and where do they fall short visually and functionally for your scholars?

3. In 12 months, how will you know the Scholar Navigator is working? Name 3 things you'd point to.
   - *Are those scholar-level signals (milestones hit, connections made) or program-level signals (enrollment, retention)?*

4. You talk about everything being "culturally affirming." What does that mean in a digital product? Give me an example of something that would feel culturally affirming vs. something that would feel generic or tone-deaf.

5. Which user group matters most for v1: scholars (9th–12th graders), caregivers, or staff/advisors? Why?

6. What should this platform NOT do? What's explicitly out of scope for now?

7. Nightmare scenario: what is the worst thing this platform could accidentally cause for a scholar? What guardrails prevent that?

**PRD outputs:** North star metric + 2–3 supporting metrics, non-negotiable product principles, explicit non-goals

---

### NC2 — Current Scholar Journey
**Duration:** 10–15 min | **Goal:** Capture the end-to-end "as-is" process so we can design the "to-be."

**Prompts:**

1. Walk me through a scholar's journey from the moment they apply to the Empowerment Program through their first month. What happens at each step?
   - *We know the Wufoo application collects grades, language preference, school counselor engagement, and areas of support. After they're accepted, what's the onboarding experience?*

2. You meet virtually 1–2 times per month for about 2 hours. What does a typical session look like? Who leads what?
   - *How do the college advising sessions (Dr. Roystone) differ from the Mosaic Mindset sessions (Elaine)?*

3. Between sessions, how do scholars stay connected to the program? Is there a group chat, email chain, shared drive, or does it go quiet?

4. Tell me the story of a scholar who really thrived in the program. What happened, and what made it work for them?

5. Now tell me about one who struggled or dropped off. Where did it break down?

6. You have about 100 scholars across 19 states. How do you keep track of who's doing what? What tools do you actually use day-to-day?
   - *We see Google Classroom, Wufoo, Zoom, and Donorbox in your stack. Is that the full picture, or is there a spreadsheet or doc that's the real source of truth?*

7. What parts of the current process would you never want to lose — even if everything else went digital?

**PRD outputs:** As-is journey map (apply -> onboard -> sessions -> between-sessions -> outcomes), failure points, current tool stack, volume estimates

---

### NC3 — Assessment, Roadmap & Personalization
**Duration:** 10–15 min | **Goal:** Define how the "know yourself, chart your path" experience should work.

**Prompts:**

1. You described wanting scholars to build a profile that captures "who they are authentically" — interests, culture, reality, presence. Walk me through what that assessment would ask.
   - *You mentioned zip code, grades, interests, and culture as inputs. How does culture show up as a data point vs. a feeling?*

2. Once a scholar has a profile, the platform generates a personalized roadmap. Give me a concrete example: if a 10th grader in rural North Carolina is interested in entrepreneurship but behind in math, what does their roadmap look like?

3. How specific should the roadmap get? Is it "take calculus by junior year" or "here are 3 free online calculus courses that match your learning style and schedule"?
   - *You mentioned wanting gamified practice modules — like Excel but more culturally affirming. What does "culturally affirming math practice" look like to you?*

4. Where do the opportunities come from? Internships, summer programs, scholarships — is Mo-Saiq curating a database, or should the platform pull from external sources, or both?

5. Milestones and check-ins: you described a checklist from 9th through 12th grade. Who defines the milestones — the platform, the advisor, or the scholar? What happens when a scholar misses one?

6. How should the roadmap adapt over time? If a scholar's interests shift from STEM to entrepreneurship mid-year, does the whole plan change?

7. The Calculus Kickstart program with The Calculus Project — should content like that live inside the Scholar Navigator, or does the platform just point scholars to those programs?

**PRD outputs:** Assessment framework (inputs + outputs), roadmap logic, content strategy (build vs. curate vs. link), milestone/check-in design, personalization rules

---

### NC4 — Social Capital, Community & Engagement
**Duration:** 8–12 min | **Goal:** Define the social layer — the "LinkedIn meets social media" experience for scholars.

**Prompts:**

1. You described wanting scholars to build social capital — connecting with people in their network who can help them. Give me an example of how a connection like that would happen on the platform.
   - *Is this mentors being matched to scholars (staff-driven), or scholars discovering and reaching out to people themselves (self-service)?*

2. You said you want this to feel like social media — the visual and behavioral aspects. What apps or platforms do your scholars spend the most time on today? What makes those sticky?

3. What would scholars post, share, or interact with on the Scholar Navigator? Is it milestones achieved, questions asked, resources shared, something else?

4. The ME2 network includes convenings, study groups, workshops, and mentorship. Which of those translate to a digital platform for v1, and which need to stay in-person or on Zoom?

5. Affirmations and reminders — you mentioned wanting those built in. Give me an example of what an ideal push notification or reminder would say to a scholar on a Tuesday afternoon.

6. You want gamification. What does a scholar earn or unlock? Is it points, badges, levels, something tied to their roadmap milestones, or something else entirely?

7. Safety: these are minors on a social-ish platform. What moderation, monitoring, or guardrails need to be in place from day one?

**PRD outputs:** Social features (profiles, connections, feed, messaging), engagement mechanics (gamification, notifications, affirmations), content model, safety/moderation requirements

---

### NC5 — Roles, Privacy, MVP & Pilot
**Duration:** 8–12 min | **Goal:** Lock down who uses this, what data matters, what's sensitive, and what v1 looks like.

**Prompts:**

1. List everyone who would use the Scholar Navigator: scholars, caregivers, Dr. Roystone, Elaine, you, mentors, partners — anyone else? For each, what should they see and do?

2. Your application collects language preferences (English, Spanish, Portuguese, Creole). How important is multilingual support for v1 vs. later?

3. You're collecting data on minors — grades, demographics, zip codes, interests. What information is too sensitive to store digitally? What consent do you need from parents/guardians?
   - *Your current privacy policy mentions sharing data with partners under certain conditions. Should the platform give scholars/caregivers control over what gets shared?*

4. If the Scholar Navigator could only do 5 things in v1, what are they?

5. What can wait for v2 or later?

6. You have 100 scholars across 19 states. What's the smallest pilot that proves this works — 10 scholars for a month? One cohort through a full cycle? Something else?

7. What does "good support" look like during the pilot? Do you need an admin dashboard, or is it more about you being in the app alongside scholars?

8. You mentioned you got some funding for this. What's the timeline and budget reality — when do you need something in scholars' hands?

**PRD outputs:** RBAC matrix (roles x permissions), privacy/consent requirements, MVP feature list (5 things), v2 backlog, pilot plan + timeline
