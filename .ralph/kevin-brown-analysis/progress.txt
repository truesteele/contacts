### Iteration 4 — US-004: Length, Format & Timing Optimization (COMPLETE)

- **short (<300) is rare:** only 3 posts — Kevin almost never writes short posts
- **INSIGHT:** Length doesn't matter much between 300-1500 chars — content quality matters more than word count

**Key Findings — Hook Length:**
- **25-50 chars is the sweet spot:** 78 posts, avg 506 — punchy but not tiny
- **50+ chars BOMBS:** avg 143-160 — too long for a hook, loses attention
- **RULE: Keep hooks under 50 characters**

- **Text dominates:** 61 posts, avg 539 vs carousel avg 345 (56% higher raw engagement)
- **BUT carousels drive more conversation:** comment/like ratio 0.217 vs text 0.171 (27% more)
- **AND carousels get shared relatively more:** share/like ratio 0.133 vs text 0.099 (34% more)
- **INSIGHT:** Text gets higher raw engagement, carousels drive deeper engagement

**Key Findings — Timing:**
- **Monday is KING:** avg 683 — strongest day by far
- **Wednesday is #2:** avg 643 — midweek spike
- **Weekend is DEAD:** Saturday avg 246, Sunday avg 145

**Key Findings — Posting Cadence:**
- **5.6 posts/week avg, median 6** — almost every weekday
- **Engagement trend FLAT across months:** 431-488 avg — no seasonal effect

**Key Findings — Cross-Tabs (the gold):**
- **advocacy_activism works in ANY format** — always high engagement
- **fundraising_strategy is better as medium-length text:** medium avg 389 vs long avg 253
- **leadership posts need LONG format:** long avg 727 vs medium avg 327
- **statistic × carousel is EXPLOSIVE:** avg 1,292 — numbers + visual = viral
- **story_opening × ANY format BOMBS:** avg 164-178 — confirmed: audience doesn't want stories

**Line Count:**
- **16-20 lines is the high-engagement sweet spot:** avg 735 (includes top post at 3,134)
- **6-10 lines is daily default:** 44 posts, avg 371

**OPTIMAL CONTENT FORMULA:**
- Length: medium (300-800 chars), go long for leadership themes
- Format: text (56% higher engagement)
- Day: Monday or Wednesday
- Hook: 25-50 characters
- Lines: 16-20 for viral potential, 6-10 for daily content

**For Kindora:**
- Default text posts, 300-800 chars, posted Monday or Wednesday
- Hooks under 50 characters — every char over 50 costs engagement
- Go long for leadership/advocacy themes only
- Use carousels when you want conversation (higher comment/share ratios)
- Never post weekends — 2-4x engagement drop
- Post 5-6 times/week — consistency > volume
- Pair statistics with carousels for viral potential

### Iteration 5 — US-005: Kevin's Content Playbook — Signature Patterns (COMPLETE)
**Script:** `scripts/intelligence/analyze_kevin_brown.py --step playbook`
**Method:** GPT-5 mini structured output (PlaybookSynthesis Pydantic model) — single comprehensive synthesis call with full analysis data as context
**Output:** `playbook` section in JSON catalog, full playbook printed to stdout

**Key Findings — Top 5 Signature Moves:**
1. **Identity Mirror** (5 posts, avg 793 eng) — Open with identity-facing statement ("Your X isn't a Y"), enumerate damage, finish with binary choice. Uses identity_appeal framing. Top example: "Your solo Communications Officer isn't a unicorn" (3,134 eng)
2. **Passionate Mobilizer** (8 posts, avg 1,216 eng) — Emotionally charged opening + tight problem-solution + direct social CTA. Passionate tone + call_to_action category = highest-engagement combo. Top example: "What has aid ever done for anyone?" (2,375 eng)
3. **Build-to-Reveal Educator** (10 posts, avg 701 eng) — Short paradox/provocative opener → staged evidence → concise lesson reveal. Exploits Kahneman's System 1/2 gap. Top example: "Simple does not mean shallow" (2,661 eng)
4. **Myth-Bust Persuader** (2 posts, avg 1,073 eng) — Strong stat/myth → systematic counter-evidence → prescriptive alternative. Rare but explosive when used. Top example: "USAID cuts $60 billion: outrage" (1,994 eng)
5. **Reframe Authority** (65 posts, avg 472 eng) — Take common assumption, reframe it, show costs, give actionable alternative. Kevin's workhorse move — 65% of all posts. Most common framing in top-10% posts.

**Key Findings — Viral Formula:**
- Theme: fundraising_strategy OR advocacy_activism
- Hook: provocative statement (25-50 chars)
- Structure: problem_solution OR list_format OR build_to_reveal
- Frame: reframing (60% of top-10%) + contrast
- Tone: authoritative OR passionate
- Length: medium (300-800 chars)
- Format: text
- CTA: none OR share_this
- Emotion: aspiration, empathy, or pride
- Devices: antithesis + tricolon + repetition as base layer

**Key Findings — What Doesn't Work:**
1. Story-first opening (avg 171) — slow starts kill attention
2. Narrative arc structure (avg 164) — audience wants frameworks, not stories
3. Storytelling frame (avg 156) — reflective tales underperform prescriptions
4. Imperative command hook (avg 236) — "Do this" feels bossy
5. Link in comments CTA (avg 294) — external links hurt algorithmic reach

**Key Findings — Audience Psychology:**
- **Identity:** Pragmatic mission-driven practitioners (fundraisers, nonprofit leaders, comms pros)
- **Triggers:** Pride (avg 1,146) > empathy (avg 798) > urgency (avg 450) > aspiration (avg 388)
- **Preferences:** Reframes, concrete steps, rules to follow — NOT stories or soft reflections
- **Sharing motivation:** Identity signaling + practical utility + advocacy amplification

**Key Findings — Content Calendar:**
- Post 5-6x/week, focus Monday & Wednesday (avg 683 & 643)
- Monday: high-impact advocacy/leadership/identity posts
- Midweek: visual creative, build-to-reveal, practical how-tos
- Thursday/Friday: fundraising strategy, curated resources
- Weekend: skip or repurpose evergreen content
- Text format default (61% of posts, 56% higher engagement), carousels for saveable resources

**For Kindora (playbook implications):**
- Kevin's top move for Kindora adaptation: **Passionate Mobilizer** (avg 1,216 eng) — outdoor education advocacy with direct share CTA
- **Build-to-Reveal** is perfect for Kindora's "did you know" format: paradox about nature/kids → evidence → Kindora's solution
- **Myth-Bust** is underused but explosive — "Screen time isn't the enemy. Indoor isolation is." format
- **Identity Mirror** maps directly: "You're not a helicopter parent for wanting your kids outside" / "Your family hike isn't just a hike"
- NEVER use story-first openings or narrative arcs — Justin's audience (parents, educators) will want the same: punchy frameworks > drawn-out stories
- Default to share_this CTA: "Repost if you believe every kid deserves time outside"
